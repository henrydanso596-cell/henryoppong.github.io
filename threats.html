  • Malware refers to malicious software designed to harm or exploit computer systems. Malware can steal data,compromise system security and Disrupt operations.

    • Types of malware includes: Viruses,Trojans, Spyware, Ransomware, Adware.

  • Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers trick victims into revealing info (e.g., passwords, credit card numbers) via:

    • fake emails: appear from legitimate sources.

    Malicious links: Leads to phishing sites.

    Attachments: Contain malware.

  • Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts files, demanding payment for decrypting keys. Attackers often:

    • Target Vulnerable systems: Exploiting weaknesses.

    Use phishing or exploits: to gain access.

    Demand ransom: usually in crypto currency.

  • Hacking refers to unauthorized access or control of computer systems,networks, or data. Hackers may:

    • Exploit vulnerabilities: wweakness in software or hardware.

    Use social engineering: Tricking users into divulging info.

  • Identity Theft: This is when someone steals and uses your personal info (e.g., name, SSN, Credit card numbers) without permission, often for financial gain.This can lead to:

    • Financial loss: Unauthorized transactions.

    Credit damage: Affecting future Loans.

    Reputation harm: Impacting personal and professional life.
